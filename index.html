Audio samples from "Crossed-lingual Multi-Speaker Text-To-Speech Synthesis With Multi-reference Gaussian Mixture Variational Autoencoders"

Authors: Hai Zhu, Shujun Liu, Huayi Zhan

Abstract:
In this paper, we introduce the Multi-reference Gaussian Mixture Variational Autoencoder (GMVAE) to improve the performance of the crossed-language multi-speaker text-to-speech (TTS) speech synthesis model. Previous works are able to model speaker and language space via categorical observed labels but there may still be many latent variations that can influence speech quality, moreover, how to adjust the strength of accent and improve speaker similarity still remains a challenge. Our main contributions include three-fold. First, we convert the pronunciation of English to pinyin phoneme so that different languages share the same phonemic representations, and regard crossed-lingual speech synthesis as a problem of language prosody transfer from multi-speaker. Second, we propose a mechanism to construct disentangled continuous space of speaker identity and language prosody for crossed-lingual speech synthesis. Third, we propose a crossed-lingual multi-speaker model that can simply control the accent strength via adjusting accent scalar. Experimental results show that our proposed model get a better performance on subjective evaluation.

We train our model on 4 open-source in the Mandarin monolingual and English monolingual dataset: (1)DB-1 is a public Mandarin speech dataset consisting of 12 hours of a single female speaker; (2)AISHELL-3 is a Mandarin corpus of 85 hours of different regions from 218 speakers; (3)LJ Speech is a public domain speech dataset that contains approximately 24 hours of a female English native speaker; (4)VCTK is an English corpus consisting 44 hours of various accents speech from 109 speakers.
